🔄 Crop Rotation

Crop rotation is the practice of growing different types of crops in the same field in a planned sequence across different seasons. It helps maintain soil fertility and improves overall farm productivity.

Why Crop Rotation is Important:

• Prevents nutrient depletion in soil
• Breaks pest and disease cycles
• Improves soil structure and fertility
• Reduces need for chemical fertilizers

Common Crop Rotation Examples:

• Legumes (beans) → Cereals (wheat)
• Rice → Pulses
• Maize → Vegetables

Benefits:

• Better crop yield
• Healthy soil
• Less pest attack
• Cost-effective farming

Tips for Farmers:

• Do not grow same crop repeatedly
• Include nitrogen-fixing crops like legumes
• Plan rotation according to season
• Keep record of previous crops
